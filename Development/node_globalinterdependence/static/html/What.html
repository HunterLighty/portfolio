What Is Global Interdependence?

Global Trade

The present global economic system necessitates trade between international partners. Every nation produces natural or manufactured goods, and exports them to nations that need those goods for necessity or luxury. Similarly, the very same nation will import the goods it needs to function properly aswell. This slowly creates a system in which nations, and by extension their people, become very reliant on other nations for the goods and services they enjoy and take for granted every day. We will go more in depth with this idea later.

Post-War System

After the second world war, the United States emerged as the sole superpower in the free, first world. They created and fostered an economic system of free, international trading that would begin to lay the foundation for the geo-economic system we enjoy today. It is strange to think that less than one-hundred years ago, nations still held colonies and inefficiently extracted their resources. The power of the United States Navy alone is to be thanked for the ability to project such safety to the world's international trade.

International Peace

What else is global interdependence? It is the idea that you and I can be apart of a broader, global commmunity of trade, that has previously been unheard of. It brings with it challenges, but also helps us explore eachothers cultures, religions, and values in constructive ways. We have the ability to use these economic connections to foster more personal ones. These can in turn help further promote peace to something not limited by the necessity of economics.
